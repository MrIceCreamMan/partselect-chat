Title: Dishwasher Not Draining - Complete Troubleshooting Guide
Category: Drainage Issues
Appliance: Dishwasher

Common Causes and Solutions:

1. Clogged Drain Hose
   - Food particles or debris blocking drain hose
   - Kinked or improperly installed hose
   - Check hose connection to garbage disposal or sink drain
   Solution: Remove and clean drain hose, ensure proper installation without kinks

2. Faulty Drain Pump
   - Pump motor burned out or jammed
   - Listen for humming noise during drain cycle
   - Check pump for obstructions (glass, bones, etc.)
   Solution: Clear obstructions or replace drain pump (Part: W10290499)

3. Garbage Disposal Connection
   - New garbage disposal may have knockout plug still in place
   - Blocks drain connection from dishwasher
   Solution: Remove knockout plug from garbage disposal inlet

4. Clogged Air Gap
   - Air gap on sink gets clogged with debris
   - Water backs up and prevents draining
   Solution: Remove air gap cap and clean thoroughly

5. Blocked Filter or Sump
   - Dishwasher filter at bottom needs regular cleaning
   - Food particles accumulate and block drainage
   Solution: Remove and clean filter assembly, check sump area for debris

6. Faulty Check Valve
   - One-way valve prevents water backflow
   - Can get stuck closed
   Solution: Clean or replace check valve

7. Clogged Chopper Blade
   - Grinds food particles before draining
   - Can get jammed or dull
   Solution: Replace chopper blade (Part: W10372113)

Diagnostic Steps:
1. Check for standing water in dishwasher
2. Run drain cycle and listen for pump operation
3. Inspect drain hose for kinks or clogs
4. Remove and clean filter
5. Check garbage disposal connection
6. Test drain pump for continuity
7. Verify air gap is clear

Prevention Tips:
- Scrape dishes before loading
- Clean filter monthly
- Run hot water before starting cycle
- Use quality detergent
- Perform monthly cleaning cycle
